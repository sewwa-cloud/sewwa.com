ChatGPT is now a standard part of how people use the web. After analyzing 17 months of clickstream data across 1 billion lines of U.S. mobile and desktop traffic, the patterns are clear.

Outbound referral traffic from ChatGPT to the rest of the web grew 206% in 2025. Over 30% of all that traffic goes to just 10 domains. And more than 20% goes to Google.

This isn't a story about ChatGPT replacing search. It's a story about how ChatGPT has become one piece of a complex, interconnected search journey—one that marketers need to understand to reach their audience effectively.

Here's what the data reveals about how people use ChatGPT and what it means for your SEO strategy.

## Key Findings at a Glance

Before diving into the details, the top-level insights:

**Traffic Growth**:
- Outbound referral traffic from ChatGPT: **206% growth in 2025**
- Over **30% of all ChatGPT referral traffic goes to 10 domains**
- **20% of ChatGPT clicks go to Google**

**Search Feature Usage**:
- ChatGPT enables search on **34.5% of queries** (February 2026)
- Down from **46% in late 2024**
- Most responses still rely on training data alone

**User Engagement**:
- **50% jump** in average queries per session (last 4 months of study)
- After 12 months of flat engagement
- Users asking more follow-up questions

**Implications**:
- ChatGPT is part of multi-platform search behavior
- Google remains a key destination
- Content needs to exist in both AI training data and web search results

## The Google-ChatGPT Traffic Pipeline

### 20% of ChatGPT Clicks Go to Google

This is the most surprising finding: **one in five clicks from ChatGPT goes to Google**.

**What this means**:
- Users start in ChatGPT, then switch to Google for deeper research
- ChatGPT serves as a discovery/awareness channel
- Google serves as the validation/comparison channel
- The buyer journey spans both platforms

**Why users switch**:
1. **Current information**: ChatGPT's training data is outdated for some topics
2. **Verification**: Users want to double-check AI-generated claims
3. **Comparison**: Google better for side-by-side comparisons
4. **Visual search**: Product images, screenshots, UI elements
5. **Trust**: Established trust in Google for certain query types

### The Multi-Platform Search Journey

**Traditional search journey** (pre-2024):
- User searches Google
- Clicks through 3-5 websites
- Makes decision

**AI-era search journey** (2026):
- User asks ChatGPT initial question
- ChatGPT provides summary/recommendation
- User clicks Google link from ChatGPT (20% of time)
- User researches on Google
- User may return to ChatGPT for follow-up questions
- Decision made across both platforms

**Implication for SEO**: You need visibility in both AI responses and Google results.

## Where ChatGPT Traffic Goes (Besides Google)

### The Top 10 Domains Capture 30% of Traffic

While Google captures 20% of ChatGPT outbound clicks, other domains receive significant traffic:

**Likely categories**:
- **Reference sites** (Wikipedia, documentation)
- **News sites** (for current events)
- **E-commerce platforms** (Amazon, product pages)
- **Developer resources** (GitHub, Stack Overflow)
- **Social platforms** (Reddit, Quora)

**Why concentration matters**:
- ChatGPT doesn't distribute traffic evenly
- High-authority domains capture disproportionate clicks
- Long-tail websites receive less AI referral traffic
- Authority and recognition drive AI click-through rates

### The Long Tail Challenge

**Traditional SEO**: Long-tail keywords drove significant traffic
- "Best CRM for small nonprofit organizations"
- 50 searches/month but high conversion rate
- Worth optimizing for

**AI-era challenge**: Long-tail traffic may decrease
- ChatGPT answers specific questions directly
- Users less likely to click through for niche queries
- Long-tail content still valuable for AI training
- But direct traffic from AI may decline

## Search Feature Usage: Down to 34.5%

### The Decline in Search Activation

ChatGPT's search feature (browsing the web for current information) is enabled on **34.5% of queries** as of February 2026—down from **46% in late 2024**.

**Why the decline?**
1. **Improved training data**: ChatGPT's base knowledge is more current
2. **User learning**: Users know when to ask for "current" vs "general" information
3. **Cost optimization**: Search feature is expensive to run
4. **Query classification**: Better at identifying which queries need current data

### What This Means for Content

**For evergreen content**:
- ChatGPT will answer from training data
- Your content needs to be in training data (pre-2025)
- Historical presence matters more than recency

**For time-sensitive content**:
- ChatGPT will activate search
- Your content needs to rank in Google
- Recency and freshness matter

**For product/service content**:
- Mix of training data and search
- Establish presence early (for training data)
- Maintain current web presence (for search activation)

## Engagement Trends: 50% More Queries Per Session

### The Shift in User Behavior

After 12 months of flat engagement (October 2024 to October 2025), average queries per session jumped **50% in the last 4 months of the study period**.

**What changed**:
1. **Users learned to use ChatGPT**: More comfortable with follow-up questions
2. **Better conversation memory**: ChatGPT maintains context across longer sessions
3. **Multi-step research**: Users conduct entire research projects in ChatGPT
4. **Comparison workflows**: Users ask for comparisons, then drill down into specifics

### Implications for Content Strategy

**Traditional SEO**: Optimize individual pages for specific queries

**AI-era SEO**: Optimize for multi-query sessions
- Content needs to answer follow-up questions
- Internal linking becomes more important
- Comprehensive coverage of related topics
- Clear hierarchy from overview to details

**Example**:

❌ **Old approach**: One page targeting "best CRM for startups"

✅ **New approach**: Content cluster
- Overview: "Best CRM for Startups 2026"
- Comparison: "HubSpot vs Salesforce for Startups"
- Pricing: "CRM Pricing Guide for Early-Stage Companies"
- Features: "Must-Have CRM Features for 10-Person Teams"
- Integration: "CRM Integrations for Startup Tech Stacks"

Each piece answers a potential follow-up question in a ChatGPT session.

## Traffic Distribution Patterns

### The Concentration Problem

**30% of ChatGPT traffic goes to 10 domains**—a level of concentration that rivals (or exceeds) traditional search.

**Why concentration happens**:
1. **Authority bias**: AI more likely to cite high-authority sources
2. **Training data bias**: Well-known sources overrepresented in training data
3. **User click patterns**: Users trust familiar brands
4. **Search activation**: When search is enabled, top Google results get cited

**Implication**: New/lesser-known brands face steeper visibility challenges in AI search than traditional search.

### The Citation Disparity

Research from other studies shows:
- **47% of sources cited by AI weren't in top 10 Google results**
- But high-authority domains are overrepresented in both
- Mid-tier authority sites may gain (AI cites them, Google doesn't rank them)
- Low-tier sites lose (ignored by both)

## What This Means for Your SEO Strategy

### 1. Dual-Presence Strategy

You need visibility in two places:
- **AI training data** (for ChatGPT's base responses)
- **Live web search** (for ChatGPT's search feature and Google)

**Action items**:
- Audit where your content appears (AI mentions + Google rankings)
- Identify gaps in either channel
- Create content that addresses both

### 2. Multi-Platform User Journey

Users move between ChatGPT and Google. Optimize for the journey:
- **ChatGPT**: Discovery, initial research, recommendations
- **Google**: Validation, comparison, current information

**Action items**:
- Ensure brand appears in AI recommendations (via authority + training data presence)
- Ensure Google presence for validation searches
- Create consistent messaging across both

### 3. Long-Form Content Clusters

Single pages optimized for single keywords are less effective.

**Build content clusters**:
- Comprehensive overview pages
- Deep-dive subpages
- Comparison pages
- FAQ pages
- Update regularly

**Action items**:
- Map your topic cluster
- Create pillar content + supporting pages
- Link internally (for AI context)
- Update at least annually

### 4. Training Data Presence

Your content needs to be in AI training data. How?

**Publish on high-authority sites**:
- Wikipedia (if notable)
- Industry publications (trade journals, news sites)
- Academic sources (cited in papers)
- Government/educational domains

**Participate in community platforms**:
- Reddit (substantial contributions, not just links)
- Quora (detailed answers)
- Stack Exchange (technical topics)
- GitHub (for developer tools)

**Create original content**:
- Research and data (gets cited)
- Unique perspectives (not just aggregation)
- Comprehensive guides (become reference sources)

### 5. Monitor AI Visibility

You can't optimize what you don't measure.

**Track**:
- How often your brand appears in ChatGPT responses
- What ChatGPT says about your products/services
- Whether claims are accurate
- Which competitors appear alongside you

**Tools**:
- LLM monitoring platforms (BrandWatch LLM, TrackAI)
- Manual prompting (systematic testing)
- Customer feedback ("How did you hear about us?")

## The Bottom Line

ChatGPT hasn't replaced search. It has become part of a multi-platform search behavior where users move between AI assistants and search engines.

**Key shifts**:
1. 20% of ChatGPT clicks go to Google—optimize for both
2. Search feature active on 34.5% of queries—mix of training data and live search
3. 50% more queries per session—optimize for multi-query conversations
4. 30% of traffic to 10 domains—authority and brand recognition matter more than ever

**Your action plan**:
- Establish presence in AI training data (publish on high-authority sites)
- Maintain Google visibility (for validation and current info searches)
- Build content clusters (for multi-query sessions)
- Monitor AI visibility (track mentions and accuracy)
- Adapt to multi-platform journeys (ChatGPT → Google → decision)

The brands that understand this interconnected search behavior will capture traffic from both AI assistants and traditional search engines.
